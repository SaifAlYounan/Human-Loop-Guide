# Replit Prompt — "How It Works" Tab

Add a new tab called **"How It Works"** in the navigation bar (between Home and Data Room, or wherever fits naturally).

## Design

Match the existing DocuSign-style aesthetic:
- Navy `#1B2A4A` for headings and primary text
- Gold `#FFC72C` for accents and connector lines
- White background, clean spacing
- No clutter — this page should feel like a premium product explainer

## Layout

### Hero Section
- Title: **"How It Works"**
- Subtitle: *"Three AI models. One steering report. Zero blind spots."*

### 4-Step Flow Diagram (horizontal on desktop, vertical on mobile)

A visual pipeline with 4 connected steps. Use icons or simple illustrations (emoji-style or SVG icons are fine). Connect them with a gold `#FFC72C` line or arrow.

**Step 1: Upload**
- Icon: 📄 (or a document/folder icon)
- Label: **"Data Room"**
- Description: "Your deal documents, organized by category — financials, legal, operational, employment, and more."

**Step 2: Review**
- Icon: 🤖🤖🤖 (or three distinct model icons side by side)
- Label: **"3 Independent AI Reviews"**
- Description: "Three different AI models review every checklist item independently. Same prompt. Different models. No communication between them."

**Step 3: Compare**
- Icon: ⚖️ (or a consensus/comparison icon)
- Label: **"Consensus Engine"**
- Description: "The system compares all three opinions. When models agree, confidence is high. When they disagree, that's exactly where human expertise matters most."

**Step 4: Report**
- Icon: 📋 (or a report/clipboard icon)
- Label: **"Steering Report"**
- Description: "A prioritized action plan: what to review, who should review it, and how long it should take. Your team focuses on what matters — not on reading everything."

### Routing Matrix Section (below the diagram)

Title: **"How Items Get Routed"**

Show a clean visual of the 4 routing levels as colored badges (same colors used in the steering report):

| Badge | Level | Color | Meaning |
|-------|-------|-------|---------|
| 🟦 | CLEAR | Navy `#1B2A4A` | All models agree: low risk. Paralegal can verify. |
| 🟨 | CHECK | Gold `#FFC72C` | Minor disagreement or medium risk. Associate reviews. |
| 🟧 | REVIEW | Orange `#E88B3A` | Significant disagreement. Senior associate examines. |
| 🟥 | ESCALATE | Coral `#E8614D` | Models disagree on high-risk item OR all flag high risk. Partner attention required. |

Below the badges, one sentence:
*"Colors belong to the system, not the models. Each model gives a plain-text risk rating. The system earns the right to use color because it represents the combined judgment."*

### "Why Three Models?" Section

Title: **"Why Three Models?"**

Two columns (or stacked on mobile):

**Left column — "The Problem with One Model"**
- One AI reviewing documents is just automation
- If it misses something, nobody catches it
- You're trusting a single opinion on critical decisions
- Same blind spots on every review

**Right column — "The Advantage of Three"**
- When all three agree, confidence is high — you can move fast
- When they disagree, you've found exactly where human judgment matters
- Different architectures catch different things
- Disagreement IS the signal, not a bug

### Bottom Section — Honesty Block (optional, can stay on Home instead)

If you want to include it here too:

Two columns:

**"What this system does"**
- Reads every document against a structured checklist
- Flags disagreements between independent AI reviewers
- Routes items to the right level of human expertise
- Estimates review time to help resource planning

**"What this system does NOT do"**
- Replace professional judgment
- Provide legal advice
- Guarantee completeness
- Make investment decisions
